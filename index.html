Do I need to be a professional host to host this trivia?

No! If you are personable and comfortable speaking in front of crowds you will be great hosting our trivia. Our included instructions on how to host trivia night will get you on the path to success!

How long does the game last?

Our games typically last between one hour and 15 minutes to one hour and 30 minutes.

Do we have to subscribe to the service?

The short answer is no. However, it will save you a lot of money if you do. Buying one-off custom trivias will cost significantly more in the long run.

Can I use the same trivia at multiple locations?

You will need to buy a subscription for each location you plan to run our trivia games at.

How do I cancel my subscription?

If you give us a try and you find out we're not the right trivia night for your establishment, look for the "Update your email preferences or unsubscribe here" link in the footer of any email you receive from Next Round Trivia. Clicking this link will take you to a page where you can manage your subscription preferences, including unsubscribing. You can also click the big button on the Subscriptions page if that's more your style.

If you cancelled after an auto-renewal date you may have more trivia games coming to you. For example: if your most recent monthly payment has been processed and you cancel one week in, you will still receive the remaining trivias each week for the month that has been paid.

To Prize or Not to Prize

What if anything should you be prizing for your trivia night!

To prize or not to prize, that is the question. My answer is whatever you, the venue or trivia host, feel comfortable with that also makes your patrons happy. I have two locations right now that have vastly different approaches to prizing and both work excellently in their respective places.

Boring Brewing has created an atmosphere of fierce, but friendly, competition between their trivia teams. There is no prize from a monetary standpoint. No money, no gift cards, no free beer, or anything like that. However, the winning team does get the traveling first place trophy presented to them by the previous week's winner and they get to keep it on their table as they defend their title of champion!

Madcow Brewing does more traditional prizing and does it very well. They give away about $30 worth of gift and spend a nominal amount of money on more physical prizing. We announce our topics ahead of time at that location and they often include film or television somehow so their owner has leaned into that for thematic prizing.

Each week we have eight popcorn containers, each with a bag of Costco microwave popcorn, and some trinkets, stickers, or the like that apply to the theme of the week. The first place team gets a $20 gift certificate for the table and an appropriate amount of popcorn containers. Second place gets a $10 gift certificate for the table and the remaining popcorn containers. And if there are any popcorn containers left I tend to drop one on a table of new players or the last place team. We're in the business of making people feel good after all!

A quick note on my own approach to multiple teams tied for second place. I prize them all out! Yes we're giving away more â€œmoney,â€ but it's a relatively small amount of actual cost to the business and there is nothing more anticlimactic than doing tie breakers for teams who are not winning the whole thing, whereas tie breakers for teams tied for first are awesome and exciting!

Long story short, do prizing the way that works best for your spot and your people. Trivia night is about having fun, growing your patronage, and as a result making you more money! If you make your decisions with these things in mind, you should reap the benefits!
